Aguipo Global South Journal (AGSJ) for the Social Sciences and Humanities

About the Journal

The Aguipo Global South Journal (AGSJ) for the Social Sciences and the Humanities is the official journal of the ALL UP Academic Employees Union of the University of the Philippines (AUPAEU) Cebu chapter. It publishes articles that advance theoretical, empirical and methodological studies focusing but not limited to decolonization, destratification and democratization of social knowledge production among Filipino Global South Scholars in the university and other institutions in the region. The journal encourages submissions that discourses on conventional scholarship, propose novel ways of understanding current social concerns and manuscripts that foster conversations across disciplines. All manuscripts are double-blind reviewed. Likewise, AGSJ publishes book reviews, reports, interviews, and commissioned commentaries.

The Editorial Board

Ian Vincent C. Manticajon

Editor-in-Chief

ATTY. IAN VINCENT C. MANTICAJON is a senior associate at the Fernandez and Associates Law Office. He practices litigation in the areas of family law, defamation law, property law, labor law, and human rights law. Aside from a law degree, he holds a master’s degree in Design from Shu-Te University in Taiwan, with specializations in user-centered and sustainable design.

He taught mass media law at the University of the Philippines Cebu and is a member of the Board of Trustees of the University of the Philippines Cebu Educational and Research Foundation.

He is a member of various civic and advocacy organizations, among them the Cebu Media Legal Aid, the National Union of People's Lawyers, and the Cebu Association of Communication Educators (CACE).

He writes a regular column named "Bar None" for The Freeman Newspaper in Cebu, a member publication of the Philippine Star Group.

He devotes a good amount of his time for the grassroots communities, and in 2014 he received a Tatak UP Award from the University of the Philippines Alumni Association Cebu Chapter for his work on social change and advocacy, particularly in empowering communities through grassroots legal education and organizing.

He is married to online writer and law student Arrah Camillia Quistadio-Manticajon.

Regletto Aldrich D. Imbong

Managing Editor (Internal Affairs)

Regletto Aldrich Imbong is Assistant Professor of Philosophy of the University of the Philippines. His research interest includes neoliberalism, emancipatory politics, Alain Badiou, and philosophy of technology.

Phoebe Zoe Maria U. Sanchez

Managing Editor (External Affairs)

Phoebe Zoe Maria Sanchez, Ph. D. is Associate Professor of History and Sociology of the College of Social Sciences at the University of the Philippines Cebu. She took up her Ph. D in Sociology at the University of the Philippines in Diliman. She is a member of the board of the Philippine Sociological Society (PSS). Her publications include: Cebu's Subnational Politics: A Survey of Philippine Political Structure and Culture, Playing the Game: Reform Politicians in the Cebu Traditional Political Field, Planning the Market and the Industrial Relations System: A Critique to the Regulation School in the Era of Globalization, Kabanata 4: Pagbuo ng Pambansang Lipunang Filipino (1745-1892)sa aklat: "Kasaysayan ng Filipinas at mga Institusyong Filipino", and Ang Pagtaksil ng mga Carcaranon sa Rebolusyon.
